# Constitution of Brazil - Summary

## Fundamental Principles (Title I)

The Federative Republic of Brazil is formed by the indissoluble union of the States, Municipalities, and the Federal District, and is founded on:
- Sovereignty
- Citizenship
- Dignity of the human person
- Social values of labor and free enterprise
- Political pluralism

## Fundamental Rights and Guarantees (Title II)

### Individual and Collective Rights (Article 5)
All persons are equal before the law, without any distinction whatsoever. Brazilians and foreigners residing in the country are guaranteed the inviolability of the right to life, liberty, equality, security and property.

Key rights include:
- Men and women have equal rights and duties
- No one shall be submitted to torture or degrading treatment
- Freedom of thought is inviolable
- Freedom of conscience and belief is inviolable
- Intellectual, artistic, scientific expression is free
- The home is the inviolable refuge of the individual
- The privacy of correspondence and communications is inviolable
- Free exercise of any work, trade, or profession

### Social Rights (Article 6)
Education, health, food, work, housing, transportation, leisure, security, social security, protection of motherhood and childhood, and assistance to the destitute are social rights.

### Political Rights (Articles 14-16)
- Popular sovereignty through direct and secret ballot
- Universal suffrage
- Voting is mandatory for those over 18 years old
- Voting is optional for the illiterate, those over 70, and those between 16 and 18

## Organization of the State (Title III)

Brazil is organized as a federation with:
- Federal Union
- 26 States
- Federal District (Bras√≠lia)
- Over 5,500 Municipalities

### Division of Powers
The legislative, executive, and judicial branches are independent and harmonious among themselves.

## The Executive Branch
- The President is elected for a 4-year term
- The President may be reelected once
- Direct election with absolute majority (runoff if necessary)

## The Legislative Branch
- National Congress (bicameral)
- Federal Senate: 81 senators (3 per state + 3 for Federal District)
- Chamber of Deputies: 513 deputies (proportional representation)

## The Judiciary
- Supreme Federal Court (STF): 11 justices
- Superior Court of Justice (STJ)
- Regional Federal Courts
- State Courts

## Economic and Financial Order (Title VII)

The economic order is based on:
- Human labor
- Free enterprise
- Social justice

Principles include:
- National sovereignty
- Private property
- Social function of property
- Free competition
- Consumer protection
- Environmental protection
- Reduction of regional and social inequalities

## Social Order (Title VIII)

The social order has work as its foundation and aims at social well-being and justice.

### Health (Article 196)
Health is a right of all and a duty of the State. The Unified Health System (SUS) provides universal and free access to health services.

### Education (Articles 205-214)
Education is a right of all and a duty of the State and family. Basic education is mandatory and free for those between 4 and 17 years old.

### Environment (Article 225)
Everyone has the right to an ecologically balanced environment, a common asset of the people and essential to a healthy quality of life.
